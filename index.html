Darren's Moisturiser Maker's Kit List

Everything you need to create your own luxurious handmade moisturisers. Follow this simple shopping list and customize your creams for different skin types.

Key Benefit: Making your own moisturiser allows you to control exactly what goes onto your skin while saving money. The plastic tubs from your shea butter purchase can be reused to store your finished product!

Essential Ingredients

Base Ingredients
  • Shea butter (1kg, unrefined organic in plastic tubs)
  • Purchase from The Soapery
  • Moringa oil (500ml)
  • Purchase from The Soapery
  • Essential oils (approx. 30ml at 1-2% strength)
  • The Soapery Essential Oils
  • Nikura Essential Oils
Required Equipment
  • Hand mixer
  • Large mixing bowl (large enough to hold all ingredients)
  • Storage containers (reuse the shea butter tubs)

Custom Formulations for Different Skin Types

Adapt your moisturiser recipe to suit different skin needs with these simple modifications:

Dry Skin
Modifications:
  • Use higher proportion of shea butter
  • Add 1-2 tbsp coconut oil
  • Consider rose or geranium essential oils
Sensitive Skin
Modifications:
  • Minimal essential oils (or unscented)
  • Add 1 tsp vitamin E oil
  • Consider chamomile or lavender essential oil (if tolerated)
Oily/Combination
Modifications:
  • Use less shea butter, more moringa oil
  • Add 1 tbsp grapeseed or jojoba oil
  • Consider tea tree or lemongrass essential oils
Mature Skin
Modifications:
  • Standard formula plus 1 tbsp rosehip oil
  • Increase moringa oil for anti-aging properties
  • Consider frankincense or lavender essential oils
Important: For all formulations, maintain a ratio of approximately 75% solid ingredients (shea butter) to 25% liquid oils for proper consistency. Always perform a patch test before applying new formulations to large areas of skin.
